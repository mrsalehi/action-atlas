ActionAtlas assesses a model's ability to recognize fine-grained actions in sports.

Abstract

Our world is full of varied actions and moves in specialized fields that we, as humans, strive to identify and learn about. To evaluate the effectiveness of multi-modal models in helping us recognize such fine-grained actions, we introduce ActionAtlas v1.0, a multiple-choice video question answering benchmark on fine-grained action recognition with short videos across various sports.

ActionAtlas contains 934 videos spanning 580 fine-grained actions across 56 sports with 1896 actions as total potential choices. Unlike most existing action recognition benchmarks that focus on simplistic actions, often identifiable from a single frame, ActionAtlas focuses on intricate movements that test the models' ability to discern subtle differences. Additionally, each video in ActionAtlas includes a unique question which helps pinpoint the action's performer in scenarios where multiple individuals are performing different actions.

We evaluate both open and proprietary foundation models on this benchmark, finding that the state-of-the-art models achieve a maximum accuracy of 45.52%. Non-expert crowd workers can reach 61.62% accuracy when provided with action descriptions for each choice. For reference, random chance is close to 21%.

Furthermore, our results show that a high frame sampling rate is essential for recognizing actions in ActionAtlas, a feature that current top proprietary models like Gemini lack in their default settings.
